Child Safety Standards

HumanityU — published standards against child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE)

Our commitment

HumanityU has zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation. No content or conduct that sexualizes, exploits, or endangers minors is permitted anywhere in the app — including posts, comments, usernames, and profile content.

Prohibited content and conduct

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) in any form
  • Sexualization of minors, real or fictional
  • Grooming, sextortion, solicitation, or attempts to contact minors inappropriately
  • Sharing personal information of minors
  • Any use of HumanityU to facilitate child exploitation

How we enforce

  • 13+ age gate: HumanityU requires users to be 13 or older. Accounts identified as under 13 are removed.
  • Automated screening: every community post and comment passes a content-moderation gateway before publication. Media uploads (photos/video) are disabled in v1 and will only return behind moderated media review.
  • Human review: flagged content is reviewed by our safety team, typically within 24 hours.
  • In-app reporting and blocking: every post and user can be reported or blocked directly in the app (long-press → Report / Block).
  • No direct messages: private messaging between users is disabled in v1.
  • Account action: violations result in immediate content removal and permanent account termination.

Reporting to authorities

Confirmed CSAM or child endangerment is reported to the appropriate authorities, including the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) in the United States and equivalent bodies in other jurisdictions, in accordance with applicable law. We preserve and provide evidence to law enforcement as legally required.

How to report a concern

In-app: long-press any post or profile → Report.

By email (child-safety contact, monitored): support@humanityu.app — subject line "CHILD SAFETY". Reports are acknowledged within 24 hours.

If a child is in immediate danger, contact your local emergency services first.

Compliance

These standards implement Google Play's Child Safety Standards policy and Apple's App Review Guidelines (1.2 — User-Generated Content), and comply with applicable child safety laws including COPPA (US) and the EU Digital Services Act. HumanityU self-certifies adherence to these published standards.
